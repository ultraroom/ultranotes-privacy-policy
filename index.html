Privacy Policy

Ultra Notes

Package: com.ultranotes.app  ·  Effective date: 16 July 2026  ·  Last updated: 16 July 2026

Ultra Notes is built around one idea: your notes belong on your device, not on someone else's server. This page explains, in plain terms, exactly what stays on your phone, what you can optionally send to your own Google Drive, and what — if anything — reaches us. Short version: nothing reaches us.

01Summary

Ultra Notes does not require an account, does not run ads, does not use analytics or tracking SDKs, and does not have a server of ours that your notes pass through. Everything you write is stored locally on your device in an encrypted-when-locked database. Two features are the only ones that ever touch a network:

  • Link previews — fetches a thumbnail directly from a website you paste, if enabled.
  • Google Drive backup — only if you explicitly connect your Google account, storing a copy of your notes in a private folder in your own Drive.

Neither of these sends anything to us. The rest of this page covers each part of the app in detail.

02Data stored on your device

Everything you create in Ultra Notes — note titles, body text, checklist items, colors, pin/archive state, and app settings — is stored in a local database on your device only. This data:

  • Is never transmitted to us. We do not operate a server that receives your notes.
  • Is not visible to us in any form, aggregated or otherwise.
  • Stays on the device until you delete it, back it up yourself, or uninstall the app.

03Locked notes & encryption

When you lock a note, its text is encrypted on your device using AES-256-GCM, with the encryption key derived from the passphrase you set (via PBKDF2, 150,000 iterations).

Your passphrase is never stored anywhere, not on your device and not by us. If you forget it, the note's content cannot be recovered by you, by us, or by anyone else.

Locked notes remain encrypted in local storage, in local backup files, and in Google Drive backups alike — the passphrase never leaves your device to make this possible.

05Google Drive backup (optional)

Ultra Notes can back up your notes to a private, hidden folder in your own Google Drive, called the App Data folder. This feature is entirely optional and only activates when you tap "Back Up to Drive" or turn on scheduled auto-backup in Settings.

What happensDetail
Where it's storedA folder only Ultra Notes can access. It does not appear in your regular Google Drive file list and counts against your own Drive storage quota, not ours.
What's in itThe same JSON export used for local backup. Locked notes stay encrypted inside it exactly as they are on your device.
Who can read itOnly you, via your own Google account authorization, and Google, as the infrastructure provider storing the file on your behalf. We do not have access to this file.
Permission requestedA single, narrow Google scope (drive.appdata) — Ultra Notes never requests access to your other Drive files.
Auto-backupIf you set a schedule (daily/weekly/monthly), a background job uploads a fresh copy at that interval, only while you remain signed in and only over a network connection.

You can disconnect this at any time from your Google Account permissions page, and you can delete the backup file itself from Drive's storage settings. Google's own handling of data stored via this API is governed by the Google Privacy Policy.

06Biometric unlock

If you enable fingerprint or face unlock for the app, this is handled entirely by Android's own BiometricPrompt system. Ultra Notes never receives, sees, or stores your fingerprint or face data — your device's operating system verifies your identity and simply tells the app "yes" or "no."

07Link previews

When rich link previews are enabled (on by default, toggle in Settings) and a note contains a web link, Ultra Notes fetches a preview image directly from that website to display it. This means the linked website receives a request from your device — revealing that URL and your device's IP address to that third-party site, the same way it would if you opened the link in a browser. No data about the request passes through us. You can turn this off entirely in Settings if you'd rather links never be fetched.

08Sharing notes

The share button hands a note's text to Android's standard share sheet, so you can send it via whichever app you choose — Messages, email, WhatsApp, and so on. This only happens when you tap it, and the note content goes only to the app/contact you select — never through us.

09What we don't do

  • No account or sign-up is required to use the app.
  • No advertising or ad networks.
  • No analytics or usage-tracking SDKs.
  • No crash-reporting services that transmit device or usage data off-device.
  • We do not sell or share your data with advertisers or data brokers, because we don't collect it in the first place.

10Children's privacy

Ultra Notes is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. Since the app itself collects nothing from any user, this applies equally across all ages.

11Your choices & data deletion

  • Delete everything locally: uninstall the app, or clear individual notes from within it.
  • Delete your Drive backup: remove the backup file from Google Drive's storage management, or revoke Ultra Notes' access from your Google Account permissions page.
  • Delete a local backup file: it's a plain file you saved yourself — delete it the same way you'd delete any file on your device or cloud storage.

Because we never receive your data, there is nothing on our end to request deletion of — deleting it on your device and in your own Drive/backup locations removes it completely.
